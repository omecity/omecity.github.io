My education and training has thus prepared me to become a computational scientist and software developer. Currently, as a PhD student my research attempts to combine agent-based modeling (ABM) and reinforcement learning (RL) in order to model complex systems where agents must adapt their behaviors within dynamic environments. This combination is especially valuable in simulating multi-agent systems (e.g., social systems, economics, or ecosystems) and policy analysis, where the interactions between autonomous agents produce emergent behaviors that are difficult to predict or control. For instance, RL can enhance ABMs by allowing agents to learn and optimize their decision-making policies over time, especially in environments where predefined rules alone may not capture realistic adaptive behavior.
My research focuses on tackling pressing public health challenges, especially in the realm of non-communicable diseases. With a robust foundation in machine learning and artificial intelligence, I aim to contribute novel methodologies that combine theoretical insights with practical applications. Alongside my research, I find immense satisfaction in teaching, as it allows me to share knowledge, inspire curiosity, and empower others to explore the transformative potential of computational science.
Beyond my academic endeavors, I am passionate about applying problem-solving frameworks to other domains, including social, environmental, and interdisciplinary scientific challenges. I believe that the same principles that drive mathematical and computational innovation can address global issues such as climate change, inequality, and sustainable development. My goal is to use my expertise not only to advance research but also to make a meaningful impact on society, fostering collaborations that bridge the gap between science and real-world applications.
